Section A: Legal Knowledge & Procedure (20%)

1 According to Section 437 of CrPC, which of the following is NOT a valid ground for granting bail in a theft case?
Your Answer:

D) The complainant has agreed to withdraw the case

Correct Answer:

D) The complainant has agreed to withdraw the case

Explanation:

The complainant's willingness to withdraw the case is not a legal ground for granting bail. Courts consider factors such as the accused's likelihood to commit another offense, community ties, and prior criminal record when determining bail eligibility.

2 In the scenario, the prosecution relied heavily on CCTV footage. Which evidentiary principle should you invoke to challenge video evidence?
Your Answer:

D) Both A and B

Correct Answer:

D) Both A and B

Explanation:

Video evidence can be challenged on multiple grounds. The Best Evidence Rule requires the original recording, and Chain of Custody ensures the evidence hasn't been tampered with or altered.

3 What is the maximum period for which a Magistrate can grant interim bail under Section 437 CrPC?
Your Answer:

B) 15 days

Correct Answer:

B) 15 days

Explanation:

Section 437(2) CrPC specifies that interim bail cannot exceed 15 days. This limitation ensures that the accused's presence is secured for trial while preventing prolonged detention without proper judicial review.

4 Which of the following is a mandatory consideration for granting bail under Section 437 CrPC?
Your Answer:

D) All of the above

Correct Answer:

D) All of the above

Explanation:

Section 437(1) CrPC enumerates specific factors the court must consider when granting bail, including the possibility of the accused influencing witnesses, the nature and gravity of the accusation, and the previous criminal record of the accused.

5 Under what circumstances can bail be cancelled under Section 437(5) CrPC?
Your Answer:

D) All of the above

Correct Answer:

D) All of the above

Explanation:

Section 437(5) CrPC allows for cancellation of bail if the accused fails to appear before the court, misuses the concession granted, or if new evidence comes to light that affects the bail decision.

6 What is the pecuniary jurisdiction limit for a Metropolitan Magistrate to try a theft case?
Your Answer:

D) No pecuniary limit

Correct Answer:

D) No pecuniary limit

Explanation:

According to the First Schedule of CrPC, a Metropolitan Magistrate has no pecuniary jurisdiction limit for trying theft cases. This differs from other courts which have specific monetary thresholds.

7 Which section of the CrPC deals with the procedure for taking evidence on commission?
Your Answer:

A) Section 284

Correct Answer:

A) Section 284

Explanation:

Section 284 CrPC deals with the procedure for taking evidence on commission. This allows evidence to be recorded by one court on behalf of another when it is not practicable to bring the witness before the court where the case is pending.

8 What is the maximum punishment for theft under Section 379 IPC?
Your Answer:

B) 3 years imprisonment

Correct Answer:

B) 3 years imprisonment

Explanation:

Section 379 IPC prescribes a maximum punishment of 3 years imprisonment for theft. This is the base offense, with aggravated forms of theft under other sections carrying higher punishments.

9 Which of the following is NOT a ground for refusing bail in a bailable offense?
Your Answer:

D) None of the above (bail is a matter of right)

Correct Answer:

D) None of the above (bail is a matter of right)

Explanation:

In bailable offenses, bail is a matter of right, not discretion. The accused cannot be refused bail if they fulfill the requirements under the relevant provisions of the CrPC.

10 Under which provision can the court direct the accused to execute a bond with or without sureties for appearance?
Your Answer:

D) Section 440 CrPC

Correct Answer:

D) Section 440 CrPC

Explanation:

Section 440 CrPC specifically deals with bonds for appearance. This provision allows the court to direct the accused to execute a bond with or without sureties for their appearance before the court.

The lights were dim, and I was about 30 feet away. I'm 70% sure it was him." Which THREE factors undermine the reliability of this identification?
Your Answer:

A) Distance from the subject, B) Poor lighting conditions, C) Witness's expressed uncertainty (70% confidence)

A) Distance from the subject, B) Poor lighting conditions, C) Witness's expressed uncertainty (70% confidence)

Eyewitness identification reliability decreases with distance, poor lighting, and expressed uncertainty. These factors should be highlighted when challenging identification evidence in court.
